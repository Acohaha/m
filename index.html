Kaamulan Festival

Ethnic cultural festival held annually in Malaybalay City, Bukidnon.

Kaamulan Festival comes from the world amul, which means to gather. It celebrates the cultures and diversity of seven indigenous groups: Bukidnon, Higaonon, Manobo, Matigsalug, Talaandig, Tigwahanon and Umayamnon. These ethnic groups once made settlements about the plains of the province.

Panliligaw/Harana

Harana a traditional form of courtship in the Philippines

Harana was a traditional form of courtship in the Philippines wherein men introduced themselves and/or wooed women by singing underneath her window at night. It was widely practiced in old Philippines with a set of protocols, a code of conduct and a specific style of music. In this endeavor, the most trusted instrument was the guitar. Its intimate sound complemented the sweetness of Spanish-influenced songs combined with the poetry and lyricism of the Tagalog language as it wafted through the breezy, tropical night.
